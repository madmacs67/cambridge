Sandra Laham, Ph.D.

Sandra L. Laham, Ph.D. is an educational consultant, facilitator and process coach specializing in collaborative change methods. She has a strong background in learning and instructional design with extensive background in online teaching and learning. Sandi focuses her practice on change processes and capacity building in individuals, groups and school systems. She is committed to working with individuals and organizations to identify solutions and develop their internal resources using consensus planning and collaborative change methods.


Sandi has worked extensively with local educational agencies, intermediate units and non-profit organizations throughout the State of Michigan. She has served as process coach to the Michigan Special Education Advisory Committee the past ten years as well as taught online for Oakland University. She has done extensive work with Macomb Intermediate School District consulting with students, teachers and parents on individual behavioral and instructional issues, with teachers and administrators on organizational, curricular and instructional issues, on the development of curricula for students with special needs, and design of training for adult learners.


Sandi obtained her doctorate in psychology focusing on teaching and learning from West Virginia University. She earned her bachelor and master degree in behavioral psychology from Western Michigan University
